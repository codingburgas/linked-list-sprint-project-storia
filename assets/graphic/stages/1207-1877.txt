After the assassination of Tsar Kaloyan, Boril takes the throne of the Second Bulgarian Empire, facing internal struggles and external threats. (1207, Boril)

Ivan Asen II overthrows Boril and becomes one of Bulgaria’s most successful rulers. Under his reign, the empire reaches its peak, expanding its territory and increasing economic prosperity. (1218, Ivan Asen II)

Following Ivan Asen II’s death, internal conflicts arise, weakening the state as successive rulers fail to maintain stability. (1241, Various rulers)

Ivaylo, a peasant leader, rises in rebellion against the nobility and the Mongol threat. He becomes Tsar but faces resistance and is eventually overthrown. (1277, Ivaylo)

Bulgaria falls completely under Ottoman rule after the defeat at Nicopolis, marking nearly five centuries of Ottoman domination. (1396, Ottoman conquest)

The Bulgarian people suffer under heavy taxation, forced labor, and religious persecution. However, national consciousness remains strong through the efforts of monks and scholars preserving Bulgarian heritage. (17th-18th centuries, Various scholars)

Paisius of Hilendar writes "Slav-Bulgarian History," a key document in the Bulgarian National Revival that inspires national identity. (1762, Paisius of Hilendar)

The Bulgarian National Revival intensifies, with increased efforts toward education, literature, and resistance against Ottoman rule. (19th century, Various revolutionaries)

The April Uprising occurs as an organized Bulgarian rebellion against the Ottoman Empire. It is brutally suppressed, but it gains international attention, leading to European intervention. (1876, Revolutionary leaders)

The Russo-Turkish War begins, with Russia declaring war on the Ottoman Empire. This war leads to Bulgaria’s liberation. (1877, Russian and Ottoman forces)
