Prince Ferdinand I | of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha: Ferdinand I (1861-1948) became Prince of Bulgaria in 1887 and later declared Bulgaria’s independence in 1908, becoming Tsar. He led Bulgaria through the Balkan Wars and World War I but abdicated in 1918 following Bulgaria’s defeat.

Tsar Boris III | of Bulgaria: Boris III (1894-1943) ruled from 1918 until his death in 1943. He navigated Bulgaria through World War II, aligning with Nazi Germany while resisting pressure to deport Bulgarian Jews. His sudden death in 1943 remains a subject of speculation.

Tsar Simeon II | of Bulgaria: Simeon II (born 1937) was Bulgaria’s last monarch, ruling as a child from 1943 to 1946. After the abolition of the monarchy, he lived in exile for decades before returning and serving as Bulgaria’s Prime Minister from 2001 to 2005.

Prime Minister Georgi Dimitrov | of Bulgaria: Georgi Dimitrov (1882-1949) was Bulgaria’s first communist leader, serving from 1946 to 1949. He played a key role in establishing the People’s Republic of Bulgaria and aligning it with the Soviet Union.

General Secretary Todor Zhivkov | of Bulgaria: Todor Zhivkov (1911-1998) ruled Bulgaria from 1954 to 1989, making him the longest-serving communist leader. His era was marked by industrialization, Soviet alignment, and eventual economic decline, leading to his resignation in 1989.

Prime Minister Ivan Kostov | of Bulgaria: Ivan Kostov (born 1949) served as Prime Minister from 1997 to 2001, leading economic reforms that paved the way for Bulgaria’s NATO and EU membership.

Prime Minister Boyko Borissov | of Bulgaria: Boyko Borissov (born 1959) served multiple terms as Prime Minister (2009-2013, 2014-2021). His tenure was marked by economic growth, infrastructure development, and political controversies.

Prime Minister Kiril Petkov | of Bulgaria: Kiril Petkov (born 1980) served as Prime Minister in 2021-2022. He focused on anti-corruption reforms and economic development but faced challenges in maintaining political stability.
