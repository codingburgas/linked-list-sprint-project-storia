Khan Asparuh (681-): Founded the Bulgarian state after defeating the Byzantines at the Battle of Ongal. Secured a peace treaty, established Pliska as the capital, and strengthened ties with the Slavic tribes.

Khan Tervel (700-721): Strengthened Bulgaria’s regional influence, helped Emperor Justinian II regain the Byzantine throne, gained the Zagore region, and played a crucial role in repelling the Arab siege of Constantinople (717-718).

Khan Sevar (738-753): Last ruler of Asparuh’s direct lineage. His reign marked a period of political instability, with frequent leadership changes and external threats.

Khan Kormisosh (753-?): Came from a different ruling family. Attempted to counter Byzantine aggression but suffered military defeats, leading to his removal.

Khan Vinekh (?-761): Pursued diplomacy with Byzantium but faced dissatisfaction and was eventually overthrown.

Khan Telets (761-765): Took an aggressive stance against Byzantium but faced internal opposition, leading to his downfall.

Khan Telerig (768-777): Strengthened Bulgaria’s stability and outmaneuvered Byzantine spies.

Khan Kardam (777-803): Restored stability and engaged in successful military campaigns against Byzantium, setting the stage for the expansion under Khan Krum.

Khan Krum (803-814): Expanded Bulgaria significantly, defeating the Avars and Byzantines. Won the Battle of Pliska (811), killing Emperor Nikephoros I. Introduced strict laws, centralized power, and boosted economic growth.
